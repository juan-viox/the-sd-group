04 · Voice & Tone

Quietly confident. Bilingual by default.

The register is one notch more refined than warm — refined concierge, not luxury-cold. Every piece of copy passes the "would Sarah say this to a family member" test.

Do

  • Lead with the family's question, not the agent's credentials
  • Switch to Spanish mid-sentence if the caller does
  • Use specific neighborhood names, median price bands, and honest math
  • Name the emotional stakes: decades, a family's life, the biggest financial decision
  • Close with a direct next step — a phone, a calendar, a form field
  • Italicize moments of emphasis sparingly, only in Fraunces serif

Don't

  • "Elevate" · "Seamless" · "Unleash" · "Cutting-edge" — AI-slop verbs
  • Lorem Ipsum or invented testimonials under real names
  • All-caps SHOUTY CTAs — use tracked small caps instead
  • "Se habla español" as a footer afterthought — bilingual is the frame
  • Emojis in UI copy (signals unprofessional in luxury bracket)
  • Script fonts to signal "feminine luxury" — let restraint carry it
